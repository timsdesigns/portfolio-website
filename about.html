About me

I'm Tim Fischer, a Berlin-based software developer and computational designer with an M.Sc. in Architecture and Urban Design from Politecnico di Milano.

After a year in .Net (mainly C#) backend development, I'm now focused on AEC tech and computational design, developing tools for 3D designers, facilitating their work on the cloud, and enhancing communication.

Background



My Journey to Becoming a Developer

Exploring the path that led me to a career in development.

Prelude

As a carpenter, I felt the urge to enhance the buildings I worked on. During my Bachelor studies, I discovered the potential of computational design tools during my semester abroad.

Masters Studies

In my master's program, I explored various fields, including material research, applied mathematics, bending active systems, physics simulation, and more, utilizing computational design tools.

This exploration covered areas such as urban analysis, structural optimization, energy optimization, seismic analysis, and 3D printing for scale models and prototypes.

Masters Work

During this time, I also had the opportunity to field-test these techniques through internships and a position as a software developer and computational designer.

This experience allowed me to develop robust workflows, create tools for reusability, and focus on effective presentation and communication.

It also included international collaborations on projects like the World Writing Museum Competition and the EVOLO Skyscraper Competition.

Coding

I delved into scripting within Grasshopper for Rhino3D and learned the C# programming language and programming in general.

ComDeCon

After my studies, I founded ComDeCon (Computational Design Consulting), where I've been involved in a variety of projects.

Projects include:
  • 3D Printing,
  • Structural Optimization,
  • Visualization,
  • Grasshopper Solutions,
  • Civil Engineering Tool Development,
  • Concept and Modeling,
  • Reverse Engineering,
  • Design Consultation and Visualization.

IASS Conference and Assassin Bug

I conducted research on Responsive Systems in Seismic Computational Design, which was presented at the International Conference on Spatial Structures (IASS).

As a main outcome besides the workflow stands now the parametric component, that produces the performing structure for testing and is now under development to be published on Food4Rhino and to be made an open source GitHub repository.

Software Development

To further my interest in architectural software development, I completed intensive classes on topics like SQL and relational databases and C# .NET development with Windows Forms.

Additional learning:
  • Data types and data structures,
  • Algorithms,
  • Unit Tests (C#),
  • Asynchronous Programming & Multithreading,
  • HTML and CSS,
  • Networks,
  • AZURE and ASP.NET Core,
  • Full-stack web development with a focus on JavaScript and various frameworks to expand my skills beyond .NET (in Progress).

My Cooking Journey

When I moved to Italy for my studies, I had big plans to start a business, embrace the culture, and master the Italian language. However, life had other plans. My classes were in English, and the campus was predominantly English-speaking, leaving me with little time to learn Italian.

Living in Lombardy, I discovered the local cuisine, thanks to my girlfriend who taught me some dishes from the south. I also explored restaurants through TheFork app, indulging in seafood and steak like never before. And, of course, Esselunga became my go-to supermarket, with everything I needed.

While cooking didn't exactly relax me, I became increasingly ambitious in the kitchen. I'd dissect every delicious dish I enjoyed at restaurants, a habit I maintain to this day. Soon, I started inviting friends over, and to my surprise, they loved my cooking. It may not be my strongest skill, but I've found a way to make it work. After four years in Italy, I suddenly could cook. Who knew? :)
